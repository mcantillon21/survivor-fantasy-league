## Design Context

### Users

Active Survivor Fantasy League players using the app on challenge night. They need to enter the current challenge quickly, understand the rules and remaining time at a glance, answer confidently on mobile or desktop, and check the live competitive picture without leaving the experience.

### Brand Personality

Cinematic, competitive, and firelit. The experience should carry the tension and ritual of real-world Survivor challenge nights while remaining an original fantasy-league product rather than reproducing official logos or artwork. Copy is concise, commanding, and useful under pressure.

### Aesthetic Direction

A dark “Night at Tribal” shell combined with broadcast-grade competition UI: warm near-black terrain, controlled ember and flame accents, crisp warm-white typography, subtle rope/topographic details, and smoky translucent panels for distinct challenges. Glass effects are reserved for active game surfaces so they communicate focus and heat rather than becoming decoration. Bebas Neue supplies condensed display drama; NB International remains the readable product typeface.

### Design Principles

1. Put tonight’s action first: the current challenge is always the clearest path through the app.
2. Make competition state unmistakable: timer, progress, selected answers, immunity, tribe, and elimination status must scan instantly.
3. Use atmosphere to heighten tension, never to obscure information or slow interaction.
4. Design for phones under pressure: large targets, short copy, resilient layouts, visible focus, and reduced-motion support.
5. Fail visibly and recoverably: show useful empty, loading, error, and retry states instead of blank screens or blocking alerts.
